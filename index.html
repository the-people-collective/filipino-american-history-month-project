What is Filipino American History Month?

Every October, the United States celebrates Filipino American History Month — a time to honor the stories, struggles, and triumphs of one of America's oldest and most vibrant communities. Filipino Americans have been part of this land for over 400 years, yet their contributions have too often gone untold.

Filipino American History Month was established by the Filipino American National Historical Society (FANHS) in 1992, chosen to commemorate the October 1587 landing of Filipino "Luzonians" near Morro Bay, California — the first documented presence of Filipinos on North American soil. In 2009, the U.S. House of Representatives officially recognized October as Filipino American History Month.

  • 🌏

    To Remember

    Filipino Americans have a 400+ year history in this country — one of courage, resilience, and survival in the face of exclusion, exploitation, and erasure. Remembering that history is an act of justice.

  • 🤝

    To Honor

    From farmworkers who built California's agricultural industry to nurses who staffed America's hospitals, Filipino Americans have contributed to every corner of American life. Their stories deserve to be known and celebrated.

  • 🌱

    To Inspire

    For Filipino American youth especially, seeing people who look like them in the story of America matters. This month is a reminder: you belong here. You have always belonged here.

History & Timeline

Key milestones from 1587 to the present day — scroll to explore

  1. Today

    4.2 Million Strong

    Filipino Americans are one of the largest Asian American groups in the U.S. Tagalog is the 4th most spoken language in the United States. Over 47% hold a bachelor's degree or higher, and more than 300,000 serve in the U.S. military — the largest Asian American group in uniform.

  2. 2016

    Filipino Veterans Equity Act

    After decades of tireless advocacy, Congress finally granted long-overdue recognition and compensation to Filipino WWII veterans who had been denied their promised benefits for 70 years.

  3. 2009

    Congress Recognizes October

    The U.S. House of Representatives formally recognized October as Filipino American History Month, a milestone in national acknowledgment of Filipino American contributions and history.

  4. 1994

    First Filipino American Governor

    Ben Cayetano was elected Governor of Hawaii, becoming the first Filipino American in U.S. history to be elected governor of a state.

  5. 1993

    First Filipino American in U.S. Congress

    Rep. Bobby Scott (Virginia) became the first Filipino American to serve as a voting member of the U.S. Congress, of Filipino heritage through his maternal grandfather.

  6. 1992

    Filipino American History Month Established

    The Filipino American National Historical Society (FANHS) officially designated October as Filipino American History Month, commemorating the 1587 Morro Bay landing.

  7. 1970s

    Filipino American Political Advocacy Rises

    Growing civil rights consciousness sparked the formation of Filipino American organizations advocating for labor equity, community rights, and visibility, building on the lessons of the Manong generation.

  8. 1965

    Delano Grape Strike & Birth of the UFW

    Larry Itliong led Filipino farmworkers in a historic strike against Delano grape growers. He invited Cesar Chavez's workers to join, and together in 1966 they co-created the United Farm Workers — one of the most powerful labor movements in American history.

  9. 1965

    Immigration Act of 1965

    The Hart-Celler Act eliminated national-origin quotas, triggering a major new wave of Filipino immigration — especially nurses, doctors, engineers, and other professionals who transformed American communities.

  10. 1956

    First Filipino American in MLB

    Bobby Balcena broke a barrier when he debuted with the Cincinnati Reds, becoming the first Filipino American to play in Major League Baseball.

  11. 1948

    First Asian American Olympic Gold Medalist

    Diver Victoria Manalo Draves became the first Asian American woman to win an Olympic gold medal, capturing two golds in platform and springboard diving at the London Olympics.

  12. 1946

    Luce-Celler Act & Rescission Act

    The Luce-Celler Act finally allowed Filipinos to naturalize as U.S. citizens. Simultaneously, the Rescission Act stripped WWII Filipino veterans of promised benefits — a wound that drove advocacy for decades.

  13. 1942

    Filipino Infantry Regiments (WWII)

    President Roosevelt authorized the First and Second Filipino Infantry Regiments, allowing Filipino Americans to serve in the U.S. Army. Their service was marked by bravery and later by the injustice of rescinded benefits.

  14. 1936

    First Woman Admitted to Harvard Medical School

    Filipino pediatrician Fe del Mundo became the first woman ever admitted to Harvard Medical School, breaking a gender barrier and going on to become a pioneering figure in children's medicine.

  15. 1934

    Tydings-McDuffie Act

    The act set the Philippines on a path to independence but reclassified Filipinos from U.S. nationals to aliens, drastically cutting immigration and stripping many of their status overnight.

  16. 1920s–30s

    The Manong Generation

    Thousands of young Filipino men migrated to California and the Pacific Northwest as farmworkers, cannery workers, and domestic laborers — forming the backbone of the West Coast agricultural workforce and laying the groundwork for later labor organizing.

  17. 1906

    First Sakadas in Hawaii

    The first wave of Filipino plantation workers (sakadas) arrived in Hawaii to perform grueling labor in the sugar and pineapple industries, forming one of the earliest large-scale Filipino communities in U.S. territory.

  18. 1900–1903

    The Pensionados

    The U.S. colonial government sponsored the first pensionados — Filipino scholars sent to study at American universities — as part of a broader effort to reshape Philippine society through American education.

  19. 1898

    Treaty of Paris / U.S. Colonization

    Following the Spanish-American War, Spain ceded the Philippines to the United States, beginning a period of American colonial rule that profoundly shaped Filipino migration patterns and identity.

  20. 1763

    St. Malo Settlement

    Filipino sailors fleeing forced labor on Spanish galleons established St. Malo in the Louisiana bayous — the first known permanent Filipino settlement in North America.

  21. 1587

    First Landing — Morro Bay, CA

    Filipino "Luzonians" sailed aboard the Spanish galleon Nuestra Señora de Buena Esperanza and made landfall near Morro Bay, California — the earliest documented Filipino presence in what is now the United States.

Notable Figures

Filipino Americans who shaped history

  • Labor & Civil Rights

    Larry Itliong

    Led the 1965 Delano Grape Strike and co-created the United Farm Workers union with Cesar Chavez, uniting Filipino and Mexican farmworkers in one of America's most powerful labor movements.

  • Labor & Civil Rights

    Silme Domingo

    Seattle cannery union leader and anti-Marcos activist whose 1981 assassination galvanized the Filipino American community and led to landmark civil rights litigation.

  • Labor & Civil Rights

    Cecilia "Cissy" Suyat Marshall

    Filipino American civil rights activist and wife of Supreme Court Justice Thurgood Marshall; worked for the NAACP during the landmark civil rights battles of the 1950s and was a vital force in the movement.

  • Politics & Government

    Ben Cayetano

    First Filipino American elected governor of a U.S. state, serving as Governor of Hawaii from 1994 to 2002.

  • Politics & Government

    Rep. Bobby Scott

    First Filipino American to serve as a voting member of the U.S. Congress (Virginia, 1993), of Filipino heritage through his maternal grandfather and a champion of civil rights and criminal justice reform.

  • Politics & Government

    Rob Bonta

    First Filipino American to serve as California Attorney General (2021–present) and formerly the first Filipino American in the California State Assembly; a champion of civil rights and immigrant communities.

  • Military

    Maj. Gen. Antonio Taguba (Ret.)

    One of the highest-ranking Filipino American officers in U.S. military history; served as a two-star general and authored the landmark 2004 report on Abu Ghraib prison abuses, demonstrating integrity at great personal cost.

  • Sports

    Victoria Manalo Draves

    First Asian American woman to win an Olympic gold medal, capturing two golds in platform and springboard diving at the 1948 London Olympics.

  • Sports

    Bobby Balcena

    First Filipino American to play in Major League Baseball, debuting with the Cincinnati Reds in 1956 and breaking a historic barrier.

  • Sports

    Raymond Townsend

    First Filipino American to play in the NBA, drafted by the Golden State Warriors (22nd pick, 1978). He later organized the NBA's first Filipino Heritage Night, which has expanded to 17+ NBA cities.

  • Sports

    Jordan Clarkson

    NBA champion and one of the most prominent Filipino American athletes today; proudly represents the Philippines on the national basketball team and is a cultural icon for Filipino youth worldwide.

  • Medicine & Science

    Fe del Mundo

    First woman admitted to Harvard Medical School (1936); went on to become a world-renowned pediatrician and humanitarian, pioneering advances in children's medicine.

  • Business & Law

    Loida Nicolas Lewis

    First Asian American woman to pass the New York Bar without attending a U.S. law school; led TLC Beatrice, a billion-dollar international food company, as CEO.

  • Technology

    Dado Banatao

    Pioneering semiconductor engineer who invented the PC chipset concept, co-founded multiple Silicon Valley companies, and is widely credited with accelerating the personal computing revolution.

  • Technology

    Sheila Marcelo

    Co-founder and CEO of Care.com, one of the world's largest online marketplaces for care services; a trailblazer for women and Asian Americans in Silicon Valley.

  • Technology

    Anne Aaron

    Director of Video Algorithms at Netflix, leading the team responsible for the video encoding and streaming quality experienced by hundreds of millions of viewers worldwide.

  • Arts & Music

    Bruno Mars

    Grammy-winning, multi-platinum artist born Peter Gene Hernandez in Hawaii to a Filipino mother, Bernadette San Pedro Bayot, and a Puerto Rican father. One of the best-selling musicians of all time.

  • Arts & Music

    Cecile Licad

    Internationally acclaimed classical pianist who debuted at Carnegie Hall and became one of the most celebrated Filipina musicians in the world.

  • Arts & Music

    Nicole Scherzinger

    Grammy-winning singer and lead vocalist of the Pussycat Dolls. One of the most recognizable Filipino American voices in global entertainment.

  • Arts & Music

    Sugar Pie DeSanto

    Born Umpeylia Marsema Balinton, of Filipino and African American heritage — a pioneering R&B and blues singer whose electrifying performances made her a legend of the San Francisco Bay Area music scene from the 1950s onward.

  • Arts & Music

    Joe Bataan

    Born Peter Nitollano, of Filipino and African American heritage in East Harlem — a defining voice of the Latin soul and boogaloo movement of the 1960s and 70s, blending Filipino, Black, and Latino cultures into a sound uniquely New York.

  • Literature & History

    Carlos Bulosan

    Author of America Is in the Heart (1946), a landmark memoir of the Manong generation's struggles that remains essential reading in Filipino American literature.

  • Literature & History

    Dr. Dorothy Laigo Cordova

    Co-founder of the Filipino American National Historical Society (FANHS) and tireless advocate for preserving and teaching Filipino American history for future generations.

How to Celebrate

Ways to honor Filipino American History Month this October

  • 📚

    Read & Learn

    Pick up a book by or about Filipino Americans. Start with America Is in the Heart by Carlos Bulosan or Patron Saints of Nothing by Randy Ribay.

  • 🎬

    Watch a Film

    Explore documentaries like The Delano Manongs or Journey for Justice: The Story of Larry Itliong, or films by Filipino American directors.

  • 🍚

    Cook & Share

    Prepare a Filipino dish — adobo, sinigang, or pancit — and share it with friends and family. Food is a powerful bridge to culture.

  • 🏛

    Visit a Museum or Exhibit

    Seek out Filipino American History Month exhibits at local cultural centers, libraries, and universities. Many cities host special events in October.

  • 🤝

    Support Organizations

    Donate to or volunteer with organizations like FANHS, the Filipino American Arts Exposition, or local Filipino community groups.

  • 🗣

    Amplify Filipino American Voices

    Share Filipino American stories, art, and achievements. Follow and elevate Filipino American creators, journalists, and artists on social media.

Monuments & Historical Places

Filipino American landmarks across the United States

  • Monument 📍 Delano, CA

    Larry Itliong Memorial Statue

    Bronze statue honoring the co-founder of the UFW and leader of the 1965 Grape Strike, standing at the site of the historic labor movement.

  • Historic Site 📍 St. Bernard Parish, LA

    St. Malo Historical Marker

    Commemorates the site of the first Filipino settlement in North America, established by Filipino sailors in the Louisiana bayous in 1763.

  • Historic Site 📍 Morro Bay, CA

    Filipino American Historical Marker

    Marks the site of the earliest documented Filipino landing in North America in 1587, recognized by the State of California.

  • Historic District 📍 Stockton, CA

    Little Manila Historic Site

    Once home to the largest Filipino population outside the Philippines; now a cultural heritage district honoring the Manong generation.

  • Historic District 📍 San Francisco, CA

    SoMa Pilipinas Cultural Heritage District

    Officially designated by San Francisco in 2016, SoMa Pilipinas is the heart of Filipino American culture in the Bay Area — home to community orgs, murals, restaurants, and historic landmarks.

  • Historic Site 📍 San Francisco, CA

    I-Hotel Site — Manilatown Heritage Foundation

    Site of the International Hotel, where the 1977 eviction of elderly Filipino manong tenants sparked a landmark tenant rights battle and became a symbol of Filipino American resistance.

  • Historic Site 📍 San Francisco, CA

    Bayanihan Community Center

    A cornerstone of the Filipino American community in San Francisco's SoMa district, serving as a cultural hub and social services center for decades.

  • Mural 📍 Daly City, CA

    Filipino American Cultural Mural

    A large-scale mural in Daly City — home to one of the largest Filipino American populations in the U.S. — celebrating Filipino heritage, migration, and Bay Area community.

  • Museum 📍 Seattle, WA

    FANHS National Museum

    The Filipino American National Historical Society's national museum, dedicated to preserving and presenting Filipino American history, founded by Dr. Dorothy Laigo Cordova.

  • Historic Site 📍 Seattle, WA

    Filipino Community of Seattle

    One of the oldest surviving Filipino American community centers in the continental U.S., established during the Manong migration era of the 1920s–30s.

  • Memorial 📍 Sacramento, CA

    Bataan-Corregidor Memorial

    Honors the Filipino and Filipino American soldiers who fought and died in the Bataan Death March and the defense of the Philippines during World War II.

  • Memorial 📍 Washington, D.C.

    Filipino Veterans of WWII Memorial

    Recognizes the service and sacrifice of over 200,000 Filipino soldiers who fought alongside the United States in World War II.
